## Adaptive Foraging Route Intelligence

**Core insight:** Squirrels cache thousands of nuts annually but rely on spatial memory alone — an AI-powered foraging platform could optimize cache distribution, predict seasonal yield, and reduce retrieval failure rates from ~25% to under 5%.
**Source:** Field observation notes (2026-01-14)
**Initial strategic connection:** Extends the platform's core Foraging Decision Support capability — the product strategy names "where to cache" as a key squirrel decision the platform should optimize.

### Original Capture
Watched a squirrel dig 6 holes before finding the right acorn. That's a 17% hit rate. Surely we can do better. What if the platform tracked cache locations, soil conditions, and competitor activity (other squirrels, birds) to recommend optimal foraging routes? This is the "assessment data" equivalent for woodland creatures — the data exists, nobody's using it.
