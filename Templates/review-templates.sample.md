# Human Review Templates

Templates for evaluating stage transition readiness. Each template has two parts:
- **Gate checklist** — Must-pass items. Any "no" blocks the transition.
- **Quality dimensions** — Directional signals rated Weak / Adequate / Strong. No minimum required — these guide where to invest refinement effort, not whether to proceed.

The agent runs self-critique before presenting. These templates evaluate what only a human can: strategic judgment, organizational fit, priority relative to portfolio, and voice authenticity.

---

## Stage 2 → 3: TL;DR → Drafting

**Question to answer:** Is this idea developed enough to produce a first draft?

### Gate Checklist

- [ ] **Core framing is right.** The Core Insight and Problem It Addresses capture what I actually mean. (If not: revise card before drafting — the draft will inherit a wrong frame.)
- [ ] **Impact dimensions are credible.** Ratings and rationales reflect my understanding of the landscape, not just research surface hits. (If not: flag specific dimensions to reassess.)
- [ ] **Strategic connection is real.** The idea connects to priorities I'd actually pitch to leadership — not a stretch to fit the framework. (If not: reconsider whether this idea belongs in the pipeline.)
- [ ] **Output format decided.** I know whether this is a strategy doc or product brief. (If not: review "When to Use Which" in incubator-approach.md.)
- [ ] **No blocking unknowns.** Open Questions are genuine curiosities, not showstoppers that would invalidate the draft. (If not: resolve blockers first — more research, stakeholder conversation, or park the idea.)

### Quality Dimensions

| Dimension | Weak | Adequate | Strong |
|-----------|------|----------|--------|
| **Strategic grounding** | Tenuous connection to org priorities | Clear connection, could articulate to a peer | Would hold up in a leadership pitch |
| **Differentiation** | Obvious/incremental, others likely thinking it too | Interesting angle, some novelty | Genuinely distinctive insight or framing |
| **Evidence quality** | Research is thin or generic | Decent market/internal evidence, some gaps | Strong triangulation across sources |
| **Clarity of opportunity** | Vague "this could be good" | Clear who benefits and roughly how | Crisp problem → solution → impact chain |
| **Excitement / energy** | Feels like obligation | Interesting enough to draft | Can't wait to see this as a finished doc |

### Decision

- **Advance to drafting** — Gate passes, quality is adequate+
- **Revise card** — Gate fails on framing, dimensions, or strategic connection. Specify what to fix.
- **Park** — Idea isn't ready and isn't worth the investment right now. Move to Archive with reason.

## Stage 4 → 5: Refining → Complete

**Question to answer:** Is this document ready to share with stakeholders?

### Gate Checklist

- [ ] **I'd send this to my VP.** Not hypothetically — literally, I would share this document with leadership as representative of my strategic thinking.
- [ ] **No unresolved markers.** No [PLACEHOLDER], [NEEDS DATA], [ASSUMPTION] tags remain, or any that remain are explicitly acceptable.
- [ ] **Open Questions are genuine next steps.** They read as "here's what we explore next" not "here's what we should have figured out before writing this."

### Quality Dimensions

| Dimension | Weak | Adequate | Strong |
|-----------|------|----------|--------|
| **Strategic coherence** | Sections feel disconnected | Logical flow, arguments build | Single compelling narrative from diagnosis to action |
| **Voice authenticity** | Generic strategy document | Recognizably mine in places | Consistently sounds like how I think and talk |
| **Actionability** | "So what?" not answered | Clear direction for teams | Teams could start planning from this document |
| **Honesty** | Oversells or avoids hard truths | Acknowledges limitations | Confronts the hardest strategic questions directly |

### Decision

- **Mark complete** — Gate passes, quality is adequate+. Move to Stage 5.
- **Continue refining** — Specify what needs work and the target quality level.
- **Restructure** — The document's approach isn't working. Major revision needed.
