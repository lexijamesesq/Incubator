# Initiative Hypothesis

We believe [this initiative] will [specific outcome for users] by [approach].

*Example:*
**We believe** enabling squirrels to search for and evaluate cache sites at the point of foraging **will** significantly increase retrieval success rates **by** reducing the gap between caching decisions and environmental data.

---

# Problem & Opportunity

Describe why users can't achieve that outcome today. Explain what makes it valuable to address, and what the opportunity is if we solve for it.

* Who struggles with this?
* Why is the current state inadequate?
* What changes when we solve it?

*Example:*

Squirrels struggle to optimize cache placement because environmental data is disconnected from their foraging workflow. Currently, they must rely on spatial memory alone, revisiting cache sites with a ~25% failure rate. Many squirrels skip optimization entirely or cache inconsistently, leading to food loss and wasted energy. When environmental intelligence is embedded in the foraging flow, squirrels naturally make better caching decisions and can reliably retrieve stores through winter.

# Strategic Anchors

Align this work with the relevant themes from your product strategy, design strategy, or OKRs (links configured in CLAUDE.md under Configuration > External References) to connect it to the larger outcomes you're driving toward. Explicitly mapping to these strategic themes helps everyone understand how this initiative aligns with our goals and why it is important. *Note: This can alternatively be a customer commit or sales enablement.*

* How does this advance our product goals?
* How does this advance our design goals?
* What larger outcome does this contribute to?
* What is the impact if we don't do this?

*Example:*

**How does this advance our product goals?**

This accelerates platform adoption by making environmental intelligence a competitive advantage rather than a barrier. When squirrels can evaluate cache sites in the same time it previously took to guess, we remove a key friction point that's preventing platform uptake.

**How does this advance our design goals?**

This demonstrates our contextual workflow integration principle at scale — proving we can reduce time-on-task while increasing quality outcomes. Success here validates the design approach for other high-friction workflows across the platform.

**What larger outcome does this contribute to?**

This enables us to achieve our goal of 70% of caches being environmentally optimized within 6 months of launch, supporting reliable winter retrieval and giving squirrels better visibility into resource availability.

**What is the impact if we don't do this?**

Squirrels will continue caching without environmental data, limiting their retrieval success. This undermines our differentiation in intelligent foraging and reduces the value proposition of our platform tools.

# What Success Looks Like

## **Success Signals**

How will we know we're heading in the right direction during solution validation and design? What feedback or signals during prototyping, user testing, or early concept validation will tell us this approach is sound?

*Example:*

* Squirrels successfully complete the in-context site evaluation workflow in usability testing without confusion
* Test participants report this feels "easier" and "more natural" than their current memory-only workflow
* Zero critical usability issues discovered that would require fundamental redesign

## **EAP: Learning Milestone**

Your solution hypothesis should already be validated before EAP. The Early Adopter Program reveals what you can't learn from prototypes — how the feature performs with real production data, what edge cases emerge, and how users actually integrate it into their workflows.

* What do you want to learn from your EAP?
* What needs to be in the EAP to achieve those learnings?
* What customers/segments do you want in your EAP?

*Example:*

**What do you want to learn from your EAP?**

Whether squirrels actually use in-context evaluation vs. continuing their existing workflow; what types of environmental queries are most common; where confusion or friction occurs with real foraging data.

**What needs to be in the EAP to achieve those learnings?**

Search functionality with top 3 terrain types; ability to attach environmental data to cache sites; basic filtering by season.

**What customers/segments do you want in your EAP?**

15-20 colonies actively using data-driven foraging, mix of urban and forest habitats.
